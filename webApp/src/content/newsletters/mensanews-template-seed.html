Nasce il SIG Progetto Impresa
Care Socie e Soci Mensa Italia,
il Progetto Impresa diventa un SIG indipendente: Mensa Italia - SIG Progetto Impresa, con lo scopo di espandere la proposta del Mensa Italia al mondo delle imprese, attraverso un graduale avvicinamento e coinvolgimento dei soci.

Lo trovate su LinkedIn. L'ingresso al SIG è subordinato alla compilazione del modulo di adesione.

Lo scopo di questo SIG è triplice:
1. Incrementare il numero di adesioni al Mensa Italia, cercando di ampliare la rappresentanza di soci e socie interessate al mondo business
2. Far crescere la conoscenza dell’associazione attraverso il coinvolgimento delle aziende
3. Offrire un servizio aggiuntivo a chi già aderisce alla nostra associazione proponendo attività legate al mondo del lavoro

Abbiamo iniziato a raccogliere le adesioni (ad oggi siamo a 80 iscritti), organizzare degli incontri online (chiamati Sinapsi, ne abbiamo già fatte 11, di cui una in presenza a Padova) e stiamo valutando come espandere le iniziative.

Vi aspettiamo. Floreat Mensa!

22.05, Trento - Viaggio nel Cuore del Rame e Serata Giochi in Birreria! 🛠️🍺
📅 PROGRAMMA:
🕐 Ore 16:00 | Visita Guidata al Museo del Rame
🕐 Ore 18:30 | Aperitivo, Cena e Serata Giochi alla Birreria Doppio Malto

La famiglia Navarini è una realtà storica di Trento: una bottega artigiana dove la filosofia produttiva è sinergia intima con l’impegno culturale di valorizzazione e riscoperta del rame, grazie all'apertura del Museo nel lontano 1970.
La bottega ci aprirà le porte al loro laboratorio e museo. Circa tremila manufatti originali in rame, provenienti da tutta l'Europa centrale, raccontano centinaia di anni di storia di questo nobile metallo: dalle corti della casata dei Medici alle tradizioni rurali dei popoli alpini.

La visita dura circa 1h e 30 minuti e si snoda tra le sei sale del museo le officine di lavorazione del rame.

Si scoprirà come veniva estratto, purificato e lavorato il metallo, le evoluzioni di forme e di tecniche nel corso secoli per arrivare al contesto moderno. Mobili, serrature, quadri, orologi ed oggetti unici fanno da cornice alla Collezione, esposta in antiche stanze sotterranee del 1600 recuperate grazie a 15 anni di scavi manuali dalla storica alluvione del rio Gola del 1942.

ℹ️ Durata: Circa 1 ora e 30 minuti tra musei e laboratori.
🚙 Parcheggio: Disponibile direttamente in loco.


🍻 Ore 18:30 | Aperitivo, Cena e Serata Giochi alla Birreria Doppio Malto
📍Doppio Malto Trento- Via del Brennero 148, 38121 Trento

Dopo la scoperta culturale, ci rilasseremo con una serata dedicata al gioco e alla buona compagnia in birreria.

06.06, Torino - Salotto “I limiti del cervello umano”
C’è chi dice che usiamo solo il 10 per cento del nostro cervello. Tale affermazione, che sappiamo essere falsa (tranne probabilmente nel caso di chi continua a ripeterla), fa comunque riflettere: stiamo davvero esprimendo il massimo di quanto ci permetterebbe la nostra struttura anatomica? Quanto ci si può allenare a “pensare meglio” prima di raggiungere limiti strutturali per la nostra mente? Sarà il transumanesimo a permetterci di superare tali limiti o ne saremo limitati indefinitamente?
Questo il tema principale del salotto di giugno, che si svolgerà, come sempre, nella solita atmosfera leggera e conviviale senza però impedirci di conversare anche di temi “elevati”.
Mettete il vostro cappello da pensatore e preparatevi a divagare tra filosofia e divertimento!
Quando: sabato 6 giugno, ore 18.00;
Dove: salotto di Jack Ponissi, via Tunisi 124, Torino;
Chi: soci Mensa e loro accompagnatori, massimo dieci persone.
Prenotazioni scrivendo a questo indirizzo email o contattando il Segretario Jack Ponissi con altri metodi.

From the ExComm – Mensa Around the Globe
I live in the United States. It’s a large country. Not the largest, but it’s pretty big. Driving from the north westernmost point of the continental United States to the south easternmost point is a journey of nearly 5,800 kilometers (3,600 miles). That doesn’t include the distance needed to get to Alaska, Hawaii, or points beyond.
Many of us just don’t travel abroad. The ability to see awesome things right at home, potentially limited foreign language capabilities, and finances may be contributing factors. A 2021 Pew Research study estimated that only 25% of U.S. citizens have visited five or more countries, and 27% have never travelled outside our own country. Personally, I’ve visited 40 countries and enjoy seeing the world. I’ve lived, studied, worked, and traveled overseas for many years. Yet with nearly 200 countries in the world, I still have a long way to go.
The international nature of Mensa is a terrific asset for our members. The first ten years I was a member, I never did anything – other than read our national magazine and local group newsletter. Maybe I was a bit intimidated, fearing that Mensa gatherings consisted of members computing asteroid orbits or something. Perhaps that actually happens somewhere, but so far, I haven’t seen it. Mostly it’s just good folks enjoying each other’s company, laughing, and learning.
Eventually I saw that Mensa in Germany was having their Annual Gathering (Jahrestreffen) a short distance from where I was living at the time. I got my courage up and decided to see what this Mensa thing was all about. I speak enough German to get by and had a terrific time. I even contributed a few correct answers during the trivia contest.
That international experience got me started on my Mensa journey. I became LocSec (President) of my local group. I later was elected as a regional and then national board member. Along the way, I continued to enjoy participating internationally. I’ve attended our European Mensa Annual Gatherings (EMAGs). I’ve attended Mensa in Germany and Mensa Canada Annual Gatherings many times. Using Mensa’s SIGHT (Service of Information, Guidance and Hospitality to Travelers), I’ve dined with members of Mensa Philippines and got pointers on cool things to see in Ireland.
Serving on the International Board of Directors (IBD) and its ExComm, I’ve been delighted to gain new friends and acquaintances from across the world. Only about 25% of Mensa members live in the U.S., meaning there’s another 120,000 out there for me still to meet. I clearly have some work to do.
There’s a wealth of international experiences for members wanting to find them. The advent of this newfangled thing called the internet is opening up ways for international participation – all from the comfort of your own home. I host a weekly virtual lunch discussion, and we’ve had people join us from as far away as Kuwait.
If you have the time and financial wherewithal, please consider venturing out into the wide world of Mensa. There are great experiences awaiting you.
I look forward to seeing you somewhere out there!
Written by Jon Gruebele, American Mensa Chair

In memoriam – Chris Leek

Christopher Leek: 1956-2026
British Mensa has lost one of its most dedicated and influential figures with the passing of Christopher Leek on 20 March 2026.
Chris joined Mensa at the age of 21 and remained a committed member for the rest of his life. Over more than four decades, he gave extraordinary service to the organisation, including time spent as a Regional Organiser in London, 25 years on the Board of British Mensa and many years as its Chairman. He also served Mensa International with distinction, including as International Chairman from 2003 to 2007
In June 2024, having stepped down from the British Mensa Board, he was appointed Honorary President in recognition of his exceptional contribution and enduring leadership. He was also made Honorary President of Mensa International.
One of his final contributions was helping to bring to life British Mensa’s online adaptive IQ test – a generational step forward that he had championed for many years, and which launched in early 2026 with the support of British Mensa Supervisory Psychologist Kristof Kovacs.
Alongside his work for the society, Chris built a long and respected career as a Solutions Designer at BT, where he was known for his diligence, precision, and deep sense of responsibility. He was, in every aspect of his life, an honest, good-natured and decent man, guided by a simple belief in fairness and kindness. He had endless time for others, whatever their walk of life, and immense reserves of patience.
In September 2023, Chris was diagnosed with glioblastoma multiforme (brain cancer). He met this challenge with characteristic determination, seeking novel immunotherapy treatment in Germany – where, having a German mother, he spent much of his youth – and survived well beyond his consultant’s expectations. To his last, he remained engaged, positive, and supportive of others. During this time, he also saw the best of Mensa – with members, including neurologists and neurosurgeons, reaching out to provide advice during his illness: bringing like minds together.
Outside his many commitments, Chris found happiness in simple, grounding pleasures: fishing on his lake, caring for his horse Echo, attending regular whisky tastings in Manchester and elsewhere, and playing chess.
Above all, Chris was a devoted family man. He is survived by Kirsty, whom he met through Mensa and with whom he shared over 42 years of marriage; their children David, Kester and Amber; his sister, Maria; and a wide circle of friends whose lives he quietly but profoundly enriched.
Chris’s legacy is one of steadfast service, intellectual rigour, and deep humanity. He will be greatly missed.
Kester Leek
Following a private funeral, Chris’s family intends to host a reception honouring him in the coming months, to give the opportunity for his many friends and colleagues to pay their respects. Please contact this email if you would like to attend.
Members are encouraged to make donations to one of the following UK-based charities, which provide invaluable support for research into glioblastoma:
Headcase Cancer Trust
University of Liverpool’s Glioblastoma (Brain Cancer) Fund
